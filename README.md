# 🏆 HackMate — The AI Copilot for Hackathons

> Practice your pitch, improve your submission, and prepare for judging with an AI-powered hackathon companion.

HackMate is an AI-powered assistant designed to help hackers navigate the most critical moments of a hackathon. While AI has made building faster than ever, the biggest challenge during a 24–48 hour sprint is making the right decisions: explaining your idea clearly, preparing for judges, and delivering a polished final submission.

The current MVP focuses on the final stage of the hackathon journey: **turning a good project into a winning presentation.**

---

# 🚀 Why HackMate?

Every hackathon team faces the same questions:

- 🤔 *Can judges understand what makes our project special?*
- 🎤 *Are we prepared for difficult technical questions?*
- 📄 *Does our Devpost clearly communicate our innovation?*
- 🏆 *Are we presenting our project in the strongest possible way?*

HackMate acts as a personal AI mentor that helps teams prepare before they step in front of judges.

---

# ⚖️ Feature 1: AI Judge Simulator

Practice your pitch against an adaptive AI judge.

The AI behaves like a real hackathon judge by:

- Understanding your project idea
- Asking follow-up technical and product questions
- Challenging your design decisions
- Testing whether your explanation is clear
- Simulating a multi-round judging experience
- Providing a final scorecard with detailed feedback

### Example Flow

**You:**

> We built an AI travel assistant that automatically creates personalized itineraries.

**AI Judge:**

> Many AI travel assistants already exist. What makes your solution unique and why would a user choose it over existing alternatives?

After a series of questions, HackMate generates a final evaluation based on areas such as:

- Innovation
- Technical Complexity
- Real-World Impact
- Presentation Clarity
- Overall Readiness

## Judge-Aware Question Generation

In future versions, HackMate can adapt its Judge Simulator based on the actual judges attending a hackathon.

Instead of asking generic questions, HackMate can generate practice questions using:

- Judge expertise and background
- Sponsor judging priorities
- MLH/event-specific scoring rubrics
- Project category and technical stack

For example, an AI researcher may ask about model reliability and evaluation, while a sponsor judge may focus on how meaningfully the project uses their API.

This makes pitch practice more realistic and helps teams prepare for the types of questions they are most likely to face during judging.

# 📋 Feature 2: AI Submission Checklist

Before submitting to Devpost, HackMate reviews your project materials.

Users can provide:

- Devpost project description
- GitHub README

The AI evaluates:

- Problem clarity
- Solution explanation
- Technical depth
- Feature completeness
- Demo readiness
- Missing information

The result is a detailed readiness report with actionable improvements.

---

# 🧠 Why Not Just Use ChatGPT?

HackMate is not intended to be another general-purpose chatbot.

It provides a structured hackathon workflow designed specifically around how projects are judged and submitted.

Unlike a generic AI assistant, HackMate focuses on:

- Simulated judge interactions
- Structured scoring criteria
- Submission-specific feedback
- Hackathon-focused coaching

This MVP validates the interaction model, with future versions expanding into a complete AI copilot for the entire hackathon lifecycle.
